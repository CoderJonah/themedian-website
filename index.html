Why The Two-Party System Fails

The Problem with Polarization

America's political landscape has become increasingly polarized, with both major parties moving toward their extremes. This creates:

  • Legislative Gridlock: Important issues go unaddressed while parties fight
  • Voter Disenfranchisement: Millions feel unrepresented by extreme positions
  • Media Echo Chambers: Information silos reinforce existing beliefs
  • Lack of Accountability: Safe districts reduce incentive for compromise

The Median Solution

By positioning ourselves in the center and securing swing votes, we can:

  • Break legislative deadlocks with practical compromise
  • Represent the moderate majority often ignored by extremes
  • Focus on evidence-based policy rather than ideology
  • Create incentives for bipartisan cooperation
